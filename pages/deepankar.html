About

The short version

I'm Deepankar Dimri. I lead growth at CubeAPM, a modern observability platform. Before that, I built growth engines at five different B2B SaaS companies across data, email, supply chain, and digital transformation.

Here's the thing most people don't say out loud: I started on the phones. SDR. Cold calls. Rejections that would make your CRM cry. And I wouldn't trade a single one of those dials for a shortcut.

That grind gave me something you can't learn from a playbook—an instinct for what real buyers actually care about. Not what your personas doc says they care about. What they actually care about.

I have a B.Tech in Computer Science and a postgrad in Digital Marketing from MICA. The engineer brain builds the systems. The marketer brain finds the signal. Together, they're the unfair advantage.

Based in India. Building for a global audience. Currently obsessed with helping engineering teams discover CubeAPM before their monitoring bill makes them cry.

Engineering The Pivot

Philosophy

How I think about growth

01

Reps over theory

You can read every growth playbook ever written and still not know how to grow a product. The only way to learn is by doing—launching campaigns that fail, writing copy that nobody clicks, building funnels that leak. The reps compound.

02

Observe before you optimize

Working in observability taught me something that applies to all of growth: you can't fix what you can't see. Before you touch the funnel, understand it. Before you scale a channel, measure it. Observation isn't passive—it's the prerequisite.

03

Technical + creative is rare

Most growth people are either technical or creative. Rarely both. A CS degree means I can read the data myself. A marketing degree means I know what story the data is telling. The intersection is where leverage lives.

04

Never outgrow the grind

Head of Growth sounds impressive until you realize the job is still about doing the work. Writing the email. Analyzing the cohort. Talking to customers. The title changes. The work doesn't. And that's the point.
